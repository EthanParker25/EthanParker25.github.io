My life is a mix of music, school, and family—and I wouldn’t have it any other way. I’m a sophomore at Crimson Cliffs High School, but outside of the classroom,
I’m fully focused on making rap music. For me, it’s more than just a creative outlet—it’s how I express who I am, what I’ve been through, and where I’m going.
Whether I’m writing lyrics, recording tracks, or just vibing with beats, I put my heart into everything I create. I’ve still got a lot to learn, but every verse,
every freestyle, every late-night session is part of my journey.

Balancing school and music isn’t always easy, but I’ve learned how to move through both worlds. In school, I’m focused on getting things done and building a solid
future. In music, I’m chasing a dream, creating something real, and finding my voice. It’s not always a smooth ride, but I stay committed, because I know who I
am and what I want to do with my life.

Family means a lot to me, too. I have two siblings who keep life interesting and always bring a sense of support and energy to my world. And something I’m especially
proud of is my roots—my mom is from El Salvador. Her story, her strength, and our culture have had a big impact on the person I’m becoming. That background gives me
something powerful to represent in my music, and it’s part of what makes my voice unique.
